Storth Cleartree

The Eternal Chronicler, The Hope-Bearer

"Every tale has worth, every bond has power, and every choice echoes through eternity. Let us write a story worth remembering."

The Making of a God

In the ancient days of The Greenwold, when the great city of Greyhaven was but a gleaming vision and the Taglebrier Forest held mysteries untold, there lived a human bard whose name would echo through eternity. Storth Cleartree was no ordinary minstrel—he was a keeper of stories, a guardian of hope, and unknowingly, the seed from which both salvation and damnation would grow.

The tale of Storth's ascension is itself a story worth telling, though the details have become legend wrapped in mystery. In life, Storth was consumed by a warforged construct in circumstances lost to time—some say it was a heroic sacrifice, others claim it was an accident of magical experimentation. What is known is that through will, magic, or divine intervention, Storth freed himself from the construct's form, though the experience forever changed him.

This transformation became the foundation of his divine nature. As a deity, Storth appears sometimes as the human he once was—warm, wise, with eyes that hold the weight of every story ever told. Other times, he manifests in warforged form, a being of metal and magic that speaks to the enduring nature of tales carved in steel and etched in crystal.

Sacred Artifacts

The Eternal Lyre

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a Bard or Cleric of Storth)

Storth's most sacred artifact is an instrument of unparalleled power. Its golden frame gleams with inner light, and its strings appear to be woven from captured starlight itself. It is said that this lyre can play any song that has ever been composed, will ever be composed, or exists in the spaces between reality and dream.

Properties:
  • Legendary Songs: The lyre knows every song ever written and can play any piece of music the wielder can imagine.
  • Hope's Resonance: When played to inspire courage, all allies within 60 feet gain advantage on saving throws against fear and despair for 24 hours.
  • Story Weaving: Once per day, the wielder can cast legend lore without expending a spell slot by playing a song about the subject.
  • Harmonic Healing: Playing for 10 minutes during a long rest allows up to 6 creatures to regain an additional hit die.

The Chronicle of All Tales

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature with Intelligence 15 or higher)

This ever-writing tome appears as a large, leather-bound book with pages that never seem to end. As mortals live their lives, make their choices, and weave their stories, the Chronicle records them all. Some say it not only records what has happened but what will happen—every possible story, every potential tale.

Properties:
  • Living History: The book automatically records significant events happening within 10 miles of its location.
  • Story Search: The wielder can speak a person's name or event, and the book will open to relevant entries (if any exist).
  • Preservation: Three times per day, the wielder can cast modify memory to restore lost or corrupted memories.
  • Narrative Insight: Once per day, gain advantage on all Intelligence and Wisdom-based skill checks for 8 hours.

Worship and Followers

Temples dedicated to Storth are called Cleartree Sanctuaries, often built in groves where ancient trees have been hollowed out to create reading rooms and performance spaces. These sanctuaries serve as libraries, concert halls, and places of refuge for those whose stories have taken dark turns.

The largest of these sanctuaries stands in Greyhaven itself, where the great Library of Endless Stories houses thousands of volumes and provides shelter for wandering bards and scholars. Smaller sanctuaries can be found throughout The Greenwold, often marked by the presence of Resonance Stones—small crystals that glow faintly and can preserve important memories or stories for future generations.

Adventure Hooks

The Silent Stone

A Resonance Stone in a remote village has stopped glowing and no longer responds to attempts to store or retrieve memories. Investigation reveals that it contains the trapped consciousness of someone who knew Storth personally—but they refuse to speak until a great wrong is righted.

The Incomplete Chronicle

Scholars have discovered that several pages are missing from an ancient copy of the Chronicle of All Tales. The missing sections seem to relate to a specific period in history, and their absence is causing reality itself to become uncertain about what happened during that time.

The Wandering Sanctuary

A Cleartree Sanctuary has begun moving through the forest on its own, appearing in different locations where its services are needed most. But the sanctuary's guardian has been having strange dreams about a story that "needs to be told before it's too late."

The False Lyre

Someone has been impersonating Storth, appearing to desperate communities with what appears to be the Eternal Lyre. While the music is beautiful and the hope it inspires is real, something feels wrong about the songs—they seem to be changing people's memories of their own stories.

Divine Portfolio

Domains: Wisdom, Light, Life, Stories
Alignment: Chaotic Good
Symbol: A golden lyre with strings of starlight
Favored Weapon: Lyre (used as club), Shortsword

Typical Worshipers

  • Bards and storytellers
  • Scholars and librarians
  • Those seeking hope in dark times
  • Chroniclers and historians
  • Anyone preserving knowledge
  • Communities facing despair

Signs of Divine Presence

  • The sound of distant music that stirs the heart
  • Books opening to exactly the right page
  • Mechanical devices functioning perfectly despite damage
  • Sudden recall of half-forgotten memories
  • Resonance Stones glowing brighter than usual

Common Prayers

The Chronicler's Vow

"I shall preserve the stories that must not be forgotten, share the tales that kindle hope, and forge bonds that will outlast the turning of ages."
