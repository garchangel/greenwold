The Divine Chronicles

of The Greenwold

In the ancient realm of The Greenwold, where heroes' choices echo through eternity and the bonds between mortals can reshape reality itself, nine divine beings watch over the fates of all who dwell within these mystical lands.

Born from the crucible of legendary adventures and cosmic conflicts, these deities represent the full spectrum of mortal ambition, virtue, and folly. Some ascended through heroic sacrifice, others fell through tragic corruption, and one maintains the delicate balance between all extremes.

The Pantheon of The Greenwold

Each deity's tale is intertwined with the others, creating a complex web of relationships that reflects the eternal struggle between hope and despair, order and chaos, connection and isolation.

Using These Deities in Your Campaign

The deities of The Greenwold are designed to reflect the complex moral choices and deep relationships that define truly memorable D&D campaigns. Each god represents not just a portfolio of domains, but a philosophy about how to live and what prices we're willing to pay for our values.

These divine beings work best in campaigns that emphasize character development, moral complexity, and the long-term consequences of player choices. They serve as both allies and antagonists, guides and warnings, reflections of what the player characters might become.
