Dongus Peardrum

The Dragon's Blade, The Fate Defier

"Every choice has weight, every action has consequences. I was meant to be a monster, but my friends showed me a better path. Now I help others find theirs."

The Blade That Chose Its Path

In the shadowed alleys and bustling markets of The Greenwold's great cities, there once walked a halfling whose very existence defied the cosmic order. Dongus Peardrum was never meant to be a hero—fate, prophecy, and the whispers of proto-gods all declared him destined for darkness. Yet through the bonds of friendship and the strength of his own choices, he became something far greater: a god who proves that destiny is not immutable, that even the most feared can choose compassion over cruelty.

Born a stout halfling in the urban sprawl, Dongus learned early that survival meant planning three moves ahead and always knowing where the exits were. His talents as a bounty hunter were matched only by his skills as an assassin—a combination that made him the most feared blade-for-hire in the known world. Those who crossed him rarely lived to tell the tale, and those who hired him never questioned whether the job would be done.

But Dongus carried more than just mortal danger within him. The strongest draconic aspect of any of the gods flowed through his veins, connecting him to ancient powers that predated civilization itself. The Bray Mother and other proto-gods whispered in his dreams, promising power beyond imagining if he would only embrace his true nature. Every augury, every prophecy, every reading of fate pointed to the same inevitable conclusion: Dongus Peardrum would become a force of terrible evil.

Yet prophecy failed to account for the most powerful force in any universe—friendship. When chance brought Dongus together with a group of unlikely heroes, everything changed. These companions saw past the feared assassin to the halfling beneath, past the predetermined evil to the person who could choose differently. Through their trust, their loyalty, and their unwavering belief in his capacity for good, Dongus learned that his flaws—his tendency to flee when things looked bad, his hesitancy born from being deceived, even his quiet prejudices—were not permanent chains but temporary obstacles that could be overcome.

The Choice That Shattered Fate

The moment of Dongus's transformation came not in a grand gesture of heroism, but in a quiet decision to stay when every instinct told him to run. Faced with impossible odds and the lives of innocents hanging in the balance, the halfling who had spent his life planning escape routes instead chose to plant his feet and fight for something greater than his own survival.

In that instant, cosmic fate shattered like glass. The proto-gods' whispers fell silent. The draconic power that had always threatened to consume him instead became his to command. Dongus Peardrum had done the impossible—he had looked destiny in the eye and said "no."

His ascension to divinity was not sought but neither was it avoided. When the forces that govern reality offered him godhood, Dongus accepted it with the same practical calm he had brought to every other challenge in his life. He would be a god not because he desired power, but because the world needed someone who understood that the most important battles are fought not against external enemies, but against the darkness within ourselves.

Divine Implements

The Blade of Choices

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature that has overcome a significant personal flaw)

Forged from the moment Dongus chose compassion over cruelty, this curved dagger appears deceptively simple until drawn. Its blade shifts between gleaming steel and obsidian darkness, reflecting the wielder's moral choices. The weapon's true power lies not in its lethality, but in its ability to reveal the consequences of actions before they are taken.

Properties:
  • Foresight: Before making an attack, the wielder sees a brief vision of the consequences of that choice—will it save or doom innocents?
  • Mercy Strike: The blade can deal non-lethal damage that still affects undead, constructs, and other creatures normally immune to such attacks.
  • Truth Cut: Three times per day, attacks automatically detect and dispel illusions or enchantments affecting the target.
  • Dragon's Precision: When fighting to protect others, the wielder's critical hit range increases and deals additional psychic damage as the target feels the weight of their own moral failures.

The Scales of Consequence

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature with Wisdom 17 or higher)

Not physical scales but a divine sense that weighs the moral weight of actions and their ripple effects through time. This gift allows Dongus's most devoted followers to understand the true cost of their choices and guide others toward better decisions.

Properties:
  • Moral Insight: The attuned creature can cast detect thoughts at will, but only to understand someone's motivations and moral reasoning.
  • Consequence Vision: Once per day, see three possible outcomes of a proposed course of action, showing their moral implications.
  • Redemption Path: Can cast geas once per week, but only to compel someone to make amends for past wrongs.
  • Fate's Defiance: Three times per day, force a reroll of any die that would result in an innocent's death or significant suffering.

The Draconic Heart

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature that has defied their predetermined nature)

The crystallized essence of Dongus's draconic aspect, this artifact pulses with barely contained tempestuous power. It represents the strength that comes from mastering one's inner dragons rather than being consumed by them.

Properties:
  • Storm's Command: Can cast any spell from the Tempest domain once per day without expending spell slots.
  • Draconic Presence: Radiates an aura that grants advantage on Intimidation checks but also on Persuasion checks when trying to protect innocents.
  • Lightning Reflexes: Grants legendary resistance (3 uses per day) and the ability to take an additional reaction each round.
  • Proto-God's Whisper: Once per week, can commune with the Bray Mother or other proto-gods for ancient knowledge, though the information always comes with moral complexity.

The Philosophy of Chosen Justice

Dongus's divine teachings center on the fundamental belief that no one is irredeemably evil, that every person has the capacity to choose differently regardless of their past or what others expect of them. His philosophy attracts those who struggle with their own darkness, who fight against their worst impulses, or who seek to make amends for past mistakes.

The Weight of Choice

Every decision, no matter how small, ripples through time and affects others. Wisdom lies in understanding these consequences before acting.

Friendship's Power

True companions see not what you are, but what you can become. Their faith gives you strength to exceed your limitations.

Practical Mercy

Compassion without wisdom is weakness, but wisdom without compassion is cruelty. Balance both to serve justice.

Defying Destiny

No fate is inevitable. The prophecies that damn you can be shattered by the simple act of choosing to be better.

The Dragon Within

Everyone carries inner darkness—greed, wrath, pride. True strength comes from mastering these dragons, not being consumed by them.

Temples and Followers

Dongus's temples, known as Sanctuaries of Second Chances, are often built in the poorest districts of cities, where his message of redemption resonates most strongly. These buildings serve multiple purposes: temples for worship, safe houses for those fleeing their pasts, and training grounds where former criminals can learn to use their skills for protection rather than predation.

The largest sanctuary stands in the undercity of Greyhaven, where a network of reformed thieves, assassins, and bounty hunters work to protect the innocent while seeking their own redemption. These temples are recognizable by their dual nature—appearing as humble halfling homes from the street, but revealing extensive underground complexes filled with training rooms, meeting halls, and sanctuaries for meditation on moral choices.

Adventure Hooks

The Prophecy Unfulfilled

Ancient texts speak of a terrible evil that was supposed to arise but never did. Scholars have traced this "missing darkness" to the point where Dongus defied his fate. Now reality itself seems unstable in that region, and someone is trying to "correct" the timeline by forcing Dongus back onto his original dark path.

The Dragon's Doubt

Dongus has gone silent, his temples reporting no divine contact for weeks. Investigation reveals that the Bray Mother has shown him visions of all the evil that occurred because he chose good—wars that started, tyrants who rose to power, innocents who died. The Dragon's Blade questions whether defying fate was the right choice after all.

The Assassin's Guild

A new criminal organization has emerged, claiming to follow Dongus's "true teachings" about the necessity of death and fear. They're using corrupted versions of his symbols and teachings to justify increasingly brutal murders, forcing his genuine followers to confront these twisted mirror versions of themselves.

The Second Chance

A legendary villain thought dead has appeared at one of Dongus's sanctuaries, claiming to seek redemption. While some followers believe in the possibility of change, others suspect this is an elaborate scheme. The characters must investigate without destroying the very trust that makes redemption possible.
