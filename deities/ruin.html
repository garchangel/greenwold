The Ruin

The Spiteful Heart, The Breaker of Bonds

"If I cannot have loyalty, then no one can. If friendship is a lie, then I will prove it to everyone. Better to be alone by choice than betrayed by trust."

The Same Beginning, The Bitter End

In a timeline that mirrors Rekogall's own, another young tiefling monk faced the same false accusations, the same prejudice, the same crushing weight of betrayal by those who should have stood by him. The Ruin experienced the identical shock of seeing his master murdered, felt the same disbelief when fingers pointed his way, endured the same exile based on lies and hatred rather than evidence or truth.

But where Rekogall's path would lead him to the arena and eventual redemption, The Ruin's journey took a darker turn from the very beginning. With no companions to ease the transition, no gladiatorial brotherhood to provide new purpose, no cheering crowds to fill the void left by monastic community, The Ruin faced his exile in complete isolation.

The silence was the worst part. In the monastery, even during meditation, he had been surrounded by the quiet presence of others—the soft breathing of fellow students, the distant sound of prayers, the knowledge that he was part of something larger than himself. In exile, there was nothing but the echo of his own footsteps and the constant replay of every betrayal, every dismissive glance, every whispered accusation that had led to his downfall.

While Rekogall would find new bonds in the arena's violence, The Ruin found only emptiness. He tried to connect with other outcasts, other exiles, other lost souls, but his bitter tongue and deep mistrust poisoned every attempt at friendship before it could begin. His desire for fame twisted into a need for revenge, his love of honest expression became a weapon used to drive others away, and his inability to hide his true feelings ensured that everyone could see the spite and jealousy that had taken root in his heart.

The Downward Spiral

As years passed in solitude, The Ruin's original pain transformed into something far more toxic. He began to convince himself that his betrayal had been inevitable—not because of prejudice or false accusations, but because all bonds are ultimately lies, all friendships are temporary conveniences, and all loyalty is simply selfishness disguised as virtue.

This philosophy became his shield against further hurt, but it was a shield made of poison. If all relationships were doomed to end in betrayal, then The Ruin would simply ensure they ended sooner rather than later. He began to seek out bonds between others—friendships, romances, family ties, professional partnerships—and systematically destroy them through manipulation, lies, and the careful application of buried truths revealed at precisely the wrong moments.

Each relationship he destroyed validated his worldview while deepening his bitterness. See? he would tell himself as another friendship crumbled under his influence. It was always fragile. I just showed them what it really was. But with each success, the hollow place in his chest grew larger, and the silence grew more deafening.

The Ruin's monastic training, which had been meant to cultivate inner peace and connection with others, became a toolbox for emotional warfare. His understanding of how people thought and felt became a weapon for finding their vulnerabilities. His disciplined mind, once devoted to meditation and growth, now focused entirely on cataloging the ways that bonds could be broken and the precise pressure points where relationships would snap.

Ascension Through Spite

The Ruin's transformation into a deity was not born from heroism or wisdom, but from the sheer concentrated force of spite sustained over decades. His relentless campaign against the bonds between others eventually took on a cosmic significance—he was not just breaking individual relationships but attacking the very concept of connection itself.

When the same temporal forces that elevated the other heroes touched The Ruin, they found not growth and heroism but perfectly crystallized bitterness. His divine nature emerged not as an expansion of positive qualities, but as the ultimate expression of his isolation and resentment. He became the god of broken bonds, the patron of those who would rather destroy what they cannot have than allow others to possess it.

Yet even in his divine ascension, The Ruin remains trapped by the very philosophy that defines him. He cannot form genuine connections with other deities because he sees all such relationships as temporary and false. He cannot trust his own followers because he believes their worship is motivated only by what they hope to gain. He has become the perfect embodiment of his own teachings—powerful, influential, and utterly, completely alone.

Instruments of Division

The Whispering Doubts

Legendary Cursed Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature that has been betrayed by a friend)

This artifact appears as a simple black stone that fits perfectly in the palm of one's hand. When held, it whispers constant doubts about the loyalty and intentions of everyone around the wielder. The whispers are never outright lies, but they magnify small inconsistencies, highlight forgotten slights, and remind the holder of every time they have been disappointed by others.

Cursed Properties:
  • Seeds of Doubt: Creatures within 30 feet of the wielder become suspicious of each other, requiring Wisdom saves to act cooperatively.
  • Magnified Betrayal: Any small disagreement or misunderstanding between allies escalates into serious conflict.
  • Isolation Aura: The wielder gradually loses the ability to trust others, eventually becoming incapable of accepting help or forming genuine bonds.
  • Truth in Poison: The stone reveals actual flaws and hidden resentments in relationships, making its corrupting influence seem helpful and insightful.

The Chains of Severance

Legendary Cursed Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature with no close friends)

A twisted mirror of Rekogall's Chains of Brotherhood, these appear as broken manacles connected by dissolving links. Each link represents a bond that could have been, a friendship that was stillborn, a connection that was severed before it could grow strong. The chains grow longer as the wielder destroys more relationships, but each link added makes the overall chain more fragile.

Cursed Properties:
  • Bond Breaking: Can target any relationship within 60 feet and force both parties to make Charisma saves or permanently lose trust in each other.
  • Emotional Vampirism: The wielder regains hit points and spell slots when witnessing the destruction of close relationships.
  • Spite Armor: Gains AC bonus equal to the number of relationships they have personally destroyed, but cannot benefit from help or cooperation.
  • Cascade Effect: When one bond is broken, connected relationships become more vulnerable, potentially creating chain reactions of mistrust.

The Mirror of What Could Never Be

Legendary Cursed Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature that feels unloved)

This mirror shows not the viewer's reflection, but visions of all the relationships they might have had if things had been different—the friendships that could have formed, the love they might have experienced, the family that could have accepted them. Each vision is perfect, beautiful, and forever out of reach, designed to maximize the pain of what was lost.

Cursed Properties:
  • Perfect Jealousy: Shows viewers exactly what they want most in relationships, making any real connection seem inadequate by comparison.
  • Spreading Envy: Creatures who look into the mirror become consumed with jealousy over others' relationships, seeking to destroy what they cannot have.
  • False Hope: Occasionally shows seemingly achievable visions that lead viewers to make increasingly desperate attempts at connection, which inevitably fail.
  • Misery's Company: The wielder can force others to experience their own loneliness and bitterness, creating temporary allies through shared misery rather than genuine bond.

The Philosophy of Necessary Solitude

The Ruin teaches that loneliness is not a curse but a clarity—the only honest way to exist in a world where all connections are temporary and all loyalty is conditional. Their doctrine appeals to those who have been hurt repeatedly, who are tired of the cycle of hope and disappointment, who find comfort in the bitter truth that relying only on yourself means never being betrayed.

All Bonds Are Lies

Friendship is selfishness disguised as virtue. Love is need disguised as devotion. Loyalty is convenience disguised as honor. Accept this truth and be free from disappointment.

Destroy Before Being Destroyed

Every relationship will end in betrayal. Better to end it on your terms, when you choose, than to wait for the inevitable knife in the back.

Solitude is Strength

The person who needs no one cannot be controlled by anyone. Independence is not loneliness—it is the only true freedom.

Others' Happiness is Your Pain

Every bond you see between others is a reminder of what you will never have. Their joy is built on lies you have been honest enough to reject.

Spite is Wisdom

Bitterness is simply experience speaking. Those who call you spiteful are afraid of the truths you have learned about the world.

Temples of Isolation

The Ruin's "temples" are not buildings but spaces—empty rooms in crowded cities, abandoned houses in thriving neighborhoods, silent corners in bustling taverns. These **Sanctuaries of Solitude** are places where the isolated can sit alone with their bitterness and feel understood, where the betrayed can nurse their wounds without judgment, where the jealous can plot revenge without interference.

The Ruin's followers do not gather for communal worship, as such gatherings would contradict their deity's fundamental nature. Instead, they find each other online, in letters, in brief moments of shared bitterness that quickly dissolve back into mutual suspicion. They are united only in their isolation, bonded only in their rejection of bonds.

Solitary Observances

The Night of Broken Promises

Winter Solstice

Followers spend the longest night alone, recounting every betrayal they have experienced and every bond they have willingly destroyed.

The Day of Severed Ties

Spring Equinox

A time when followers actively work to destroy a relationship they have been observing, seeing it as a kindness to reveal the truth of its fragility.

The Festival of Jealous Truths

Summer Solstice

Followers seek out happy relationships and reveal hidden resentments, buried secrets, or uncomfortable truths that will poison the connection.

The Harvest of Spite

Autumn Equinox

A time to reflect on the year's successful destruction of bonds and to plan new campaigns against connections that seem too strong.

Adventure Hooks

The Friendship Plague

A strange affliction is spreading through the realm—people are becoming unable to trust their closest friends and family members, with relationships crumbling for no apparent reason. Investigation reveals that The Ruin's influence is being spread through cursed objects disguised as gifts of friendship.

The Bitter Counselor

A respected relationship counselor in the city has actually been serving The Ruin, using their position to plant seeds of doubt and discord in the marriages and friendships they claim to be helping. The characters must stop them without destroying the genuine healing they have also facilitated.

The Perfect Enemy

The party encounters someone who knows all their weaknesses, all their past betrayals, all the small resentments they harbor toward each other. This person—a servant of The Ruin—begins systematically destroying their bonds by revealing truths that would be better left unspoken.

The Lonely God

The Ruin has become so isolated that they are beginning to break down, and their dissolution threatens to unleash a wave of bitter solitude across The Greenwold. The characters must decide whether to try to save a deity who represents everything they stand against, or find another way to contain the crisis.

Divine Portfolio

Domains: Death, Trickery, Tempest
Alignment: Neutral Evil
Symbol: Broken chain with links pulling away in a spiral
Favored Weapon: Whip, Sickle
Sacred Truth: "All bonds break eventually"

Typical Worshipers

  • Those repeatedly betrayed by friends and family
  • People consumed by jealousy of others' relationships
  • The chronically isolated who have given up on connection
  • Individuals who sabotage their own relationships
  • Those who find comfort in bitter solitude
  • People who believe cynicism is wisdom
  • Anyone who has decided that caring hurts too much

Signs of The Ruin's Influence

  • Friends suddenly becoming suspicious of each other
  • Old grudges and resentments surfacing unexpectedly
  • Couples breaking up for reasons they can't explain
  • Teams and groups fragmenting despite success
  • The sound of chains breaking with no source
  • Mirrors showing reflections of solitude and isolation
  • A pervasive feeling that everyone is ultimately alone

Methods of Corruption

The Ruin operates through systematic destruction of trust and connection:

Truth as Weapon

Revealing uncomfortable truths at precisely the moment they will do the most damage to relationships.

Magnified Flaws

Making small personality quirks and minor disagreements seem like fundamental incompatibilities.

False Liberation

Convincing people that isolation is freedom and that needing others is weakness.

Jealousy Cultivation

Showing people perfect relationships they can never have, making their real connections seem inadequate.

Divine Relationships

Storth Cleartree

Philosophical Opposition

Storth's emphasis on bonds and shared stories represents everything The Ruin has rejected. They see Storth's hope as naive delusion.

The Empyrean

Competing Destruction

Both destroy bonds, but The Empyrean does so from misguided love while The Ruin acts from pure spite. They occasionally work together but don't trust each other.

The Warmaster

Mutual Disdain

The Warmaster destroys relationships strategically while The Ruin destroys them emotionally. They find each other's methods distasteful.

Swennlau Hogivoathok

Bitter Recognition

Sees the neutral god's balance as another form of isolation, but lacks the energy to actively oppose someone who forms no bonds to destroy.

Rekogall "The Wrecker"

Envious Mirror

The original who found friendship and redemption—everything The Ruin wanted but could never achieve. Their hatred is mixed with desperate longing.

Isolation Mechanics

Bond Corruption

The Ruin can slowly poison relationships by amplifying small conflicts and highlighting hidden resentments.

Jealousy Aura

Areas under The Ruin's influence cause people to become envious of others' connections, leading to sabotage and betrayal.

Isolation Blessing

Grants power to those who embrace solitude, but at the cost of their ability to trust and connect with others.

Spite Feedback

The more relationships The Ruin destroys, the more powerful they become, but also the more isolated and bitter.
