Rekogall "The Wrecker"

The Bond-Forger, The Arena's Brother

"I judge people by their actions, not their words. And the greatest action is standing by someone when the whole world tells you to walk away."

The False Exile

In the mountain monasteries where discipline and inner peace are forged through years of patient training, there once lived a young tiefling monk whose silver eyes burned with determination and whose sharp tongue earned him both friends and enemies in equal measure. Rekogall was never the most patient student—his desire for recognition, his inability to hide his true feelings, and his love of a well-crafted insult made him stand out in a community that valued quiet contemplation and humble service.

But beneath his brash exterior beat the heart of someone who judged others by their actions rather than their words, who valued honesty above politeness, and who understood that true art—whether martial or spiritual—must come from within. His master saw this potential and worked patiently to channel Rekogall's fierce energy into the disciplined techniques of the Open Palm tradition.

That careful cultivation came to a violent end the day Rekogall's master was found dead, killed by a rival monk known as Lifetaker. In the chaos and grief that followed, accusations flew, and Rekogall—whose tiefling heritage and sharp tongue had already made him enemies among the more conservative members of the order—found himself branded as the killer. Despite his protests, despite his grief, despite the bonds he thought he had forged with his fellow monks, he was cast out in shame and disgrace.

The exile broke something in Rekogall's spirit. The monastery had been his home, his master his guide, his fellow students his family. To have all of that stripped away based on lies and prejudice left him hollow and angry. Unable to return to his past and unwilling to simply disappear, he found himself drawn to the gladiatorial arenas—places where strength mattered more than reputation, where the crowd's favor could be earned through action rather than words, and where a tiefling with impressive combat skills could make a name for himself.

The Forge of Combat

The gladiatorial arena became Rekogall's new monastery, and violence his new meditation. But even in this bloody environment, his monk training showed through. Where other gladiators relied on brute force or flashy showmanship, Rekogall combined monastic discipline with arena savagery, creating a fighting style that was both beautiful and devastating.

His tiefling heritage, which had been a source of suspicion in the monastery, became an asset in the arena. The crowds loved his exotic appearance, his silver eyes that seemed to glow with inner fire, and his ability to deliver cutting insults while breaking bones with equal precision. He earned the nickname "The Wrecker" not just for his ability to destroy opponents, but for his talent at dismantling their confidence with words before finishing them with fists.

Yet even as he found success and fame in the arena, Rekogall carried the pain of his exile like a physical wound. The bonds he had lost haunted his victories, and the knowledge that he had been betrayed by those he trusted made it difficult to form new connections. He began to withdraw into himself, using his sharp tongue as a weapon to keep others at distance, afraid to risk the pain of betrayal again.

But fate had other plans. During one particularly brutal tournament, Rekogall found himself fighting alongside other gladiators against overwhelming odds—a spectacle designed to showcase the arena's newest monsters. In that moment, facing certain death, he made a choice that would change everything. Instead of fighting for his own survival and glory, he fought to protect his fellow gladiators. He used his monk training not to win fame, but to forge bonds of mutual protection in the crucible of combat.

The Return to Truth

The bonds forged in that arena battle led to friendships that transcended the gladiatorial ring. When Rekogall's companions learned of his past, they helped him investigate the truth of his master's death. Together, they uncovered evidence that Lifetaker had not only murdered Rekogall's master but had also orchestrated the frame that led to his exile.

The return to his monastery was not a triumphant vindication but a humbling experience. Many of those who had cast him out were dead or gone, and those who remained were forced to confront their own prejudices and hasty judgments. But Rekogall had changed as well. The arena had taught him that bonds forged in adversity were stronger than those built in comfort, and his companions had shown him that loyalty was something you earned through actions, not words.

He did not return to his old life but instead forged a new path. The monastery became his base, but his true calling lay in teaching others how to build the kinds of bonds that could survive betrayal, overcome prejudice, and endure hardship. His divine ascension came not through a single heroic act, but through a lifetime of choosing to stand by others when it would have been easier to walk away, of building bridges when others built walls, of turning the destructive impulse to "wreck" into the creative power to forge lasting connections.

Tools of Connection

The Chains of Brotherhood

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature with loyal allies)

These appear as simple iron manacles when first found, but when worn by someone who truly values their companions, they transform into interlocked chains of iron and gold that wrap around the wearer's arms. The chains represent the bonds between allies—strong individually, but unbreakable when linked together. They grow longer and more ornate as the wearer's relationships deepen.

Properties:
  • Unbreakable Bond: While wearing the chains, the user and any ally within 30 feet cannot be forced to act against each other by any means.
  • Shared Strength: When an ally within 60 feet takes damage, the wearer can choose to take half that damage instead, distributed among willing allies.
  • Chain Strike: The chains can extend up to 30 feet to strike enemies or pull allies to safety, dealing damage equal to the number of true friends the wearer has.
  • Brother's Keeper: Once per day, can instantly teleport to the side of an ally in mortal danger, regardless of distance or obstacles.

The Arena's Heart

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature that has fought for others)

This appears as a simple brass medallion bearing the scars of countless arena battles, but it pulses with the heartbeat of every crowd that ever cheered for Rekogall. It represents the transformation of violence from selfish to selfless, from destruction to protection. When activated, the medallion projects the roar of an approving crowd that strengthens allies and intimidates enemies.

Properties:
  • Crowd's Favor: Allies within 60 feet gain advantage on all rolls when protecting innocents or fighting alongside trusted companions.
  • Arena Discipline: The wearer can enter a state where they take half damage from all sources but can only use their abilities to protect or aid others.
  • Gladiator's Resilience: Once per day, when reduced to 0 hit points while defending an ally, immediately regain half maximum hit points and gain resistance to all damage for 1 minute.
  • Champion's Challenge: Can force enemies to focus their attacks on the wearer instead of the wearer's allies, while gaining temporary hit points for each ally protected this way.

The Master's Memory

Legendary Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature seeking to honor a mentor)

This simple prayer wheel contains not words but memories—every lesson Rekogall's master taught about inner strength, every moment of guidance, every expression of faith in his potential. It serves as a reminder that the bonds between teacher and student can transcend death, betrayal, and time itself. When spun, it whispers wisdom in the voice of those who believed in the wielder when they couldn't believe in themselves.

Properties:
  • Master's Guidance: Provides advantage on Wisdom saves and grants proficiency in Insight if not already proficient, double proficiency if already proficient.
  • Teaching Moment: Once per day, can share a piece of hard-won wisdom with an ally, granting them the benefits of a long rest without actually resting.
  • Legacy of Learning: When the wielder teaches someone a skill or helps them grow, both teacher and student gain permanent benefits to related abilities.
  • Unbroken Chain: Can communicate with the spirits of mentors and teachers, gaining access to knowledge and wisdom from beyond death.

The Way of Forged Bonds

Rekogall's divine teachings center on the belief that the strongest bonds are not those inherited by blood or granted by circumstance, but those forged through shared struggle, mutual choice, and consistent action. His philosophy attracts those who have been betrayed, abandoned, or cast out—showing them that new families can be chosen and bonds can be built stronger than those that were broken.

Actions Over Words

Trust is not built through promises but through consistent behavior. Judge people by what they do when no one is watching, not what they say when everyone is listening.

Strength in Struggle

Bonds forged in adversity are stronger than those formed in comfort. The friend who stands by you in failure is worth more than a hundred who celebrate your success.

Honest Expression

True friendship can survive harsh words spoken in love. Better to hurt with truth than to comfort with lies, for genuine bonds require genuine understanding.

Chosen Family

The family you choose is often stronger than the family you inherit. Blood may be thicker than water, but loyalty is thicker than blood.

Protective Loyalty

True loyalty means being willing to fight for your friends even when they cannot fight for themselves, to stand up for their honor even in their absence.

Halls of Brotherhood

Rekogall's temples, known as **Fellowship Halls**, are designed as gathering places where bonds can be forged and tested. These buildings typically feature training areas where people can spar and challenge each other, common rooms where stories are shared and friendships deepened, and meditation spaces where individuals can reflect on their relationships and commitments.

The largest Fellowship Hall stands in the rebuilt section of his original monastery, where former gladiators train alongside monks, and where people of all backgrounds come to learn that strength shared is strength multiplied. These halls are recognizable by their circular architecture—no corners where someone can be isolated, no high seats where some are elevated above others, just shared space where all can meet as equals.

Adventure Hooks

The Broken Fellowship

A group of heroes who once saved the kingdom together have turned against each other, their bonds shattered by secrets and betrayals. Rekogall's clerics believe only by recreating the original circumstances of their bonding can their fellowship be restored—but that means facing the same dangers that originally united them.

The False Brother

Someone claiming to be a lost member of the party's group has appeared, with memories and knowledge that seem genuine. But their presence is slowly destroying the bonds between the real companions, and investigation suggests they may be a creation of The Ruin, designed to tear apart strong friendships from within.

The Loyalty Test

A trusted ally has been accused of terrible crimes, and all evidence points to their guilt. The characters must choose between loyalty to their friend and justice for the victims—but Rekogall's teachings suggest that true loyalty sometimes means holding friends accountable, not blindly defending them.

The Arena of Souls

A mysterious gladiatorial tournament has appeared in the city, where combatants fight not for gold but to prove the strength of their bonds. Pairs who trust each other completely can achieve impossible victories, while those with doubts find their relationships literally torn apart in the arena's supernatural combat.

Divine Portfolio

Domains: Conflict, Defense, Death, Fire
Alignment: Neutral Good
Symbol: Interlocked chains of iron and gold forming a circle
Favored Weapon: Unarmed strikes, Chain weapons
Sacred Vow: "I stand by those who stand by me"

Typical Worshipers

  • Those falsely accused or betrayed by former allies
  • Gladiators, fighters, and martial artists
  • People seeking to build new families after loss
  • Guards and soldiers who protect their brothers-in-arms
  • Former outcasts who found acceptance
  • Anyone who values loyalty above convenience
  • Communities facing external threats requiring unity

Signs of Divine Presence

  • Chains that cannot be broken when protecting allies
  • The distant roar of an approving crowd during heroic acts
  • Enemies finding it impossible to separate close companions
  • Wounds healing faster when tended by trusted friends
  • Lost friends finding each other against impossible odds
  • Training equipment that grows stronger with each partner

"I will stand when others fall, fight when others flee, and hold the line when all seems lost—not for glory, but for those who stand beside me."

The Fellowship Blessing

"May our differences become strengths, our individual talents serve our common cause, and our shared struggles forge bonds that neither time nor betrayal can break."

Expanded Domain Features

War Domain

Focuses on coordinated combat and protecting allies. Gains bonuses when fighting alongside trusted companions and can share combat abilities with them.

Tempest Domain

Storm powers that strengthen when defending friends. Lightning that arcs between allies to heal them and between enemies to chain damage.

Protection Domain

Enhanced ability to shield others and take damage meant for allies. Can extend protective magic to cover entire groups of bonded companions.
