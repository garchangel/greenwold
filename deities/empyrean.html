The Empyrean

The Fallen Mirror, The Betrayer's Crown

"Sometimes the only way to save what you love is to destroy it before someone else can. I made the hard choices others wouldn't make, and for that, I am forever damned."

The Mirror's Creation

In the darkest chapters of The Greenwold's history, there exists a tale of such tragedy that many scholars refuse to record it, and those who do speak of it only in whispers. It is the story of The Empyrean—a being who began as a reflection of hope and became the embodiment of sacrificial despair, a god whose very existence serves as a warning about the prices we pay for the choices we make in desperate times.

The Empyrean was never meant to exist. Born from a catastrophic chronomancy spell that sought to peer through time's veil, The Empyrean emerged as a dark reflection of Storth Cleartree—not his opposite, but his shadow, possessing all of Storth's potential twisted into forms that serve darker purposes. Where Storth preserved stories, The Empyrean collected secrets. Where Storth inspired hope, The Empyrean whispered of necessary sacrifices.

The spell that created this cosmic error occurred during a period of great crisis in The Greenwold, when heroes were desperately seeking knowledge that might save their world from an unnamed threat. In their pursuit of temporal wisdom, they unknowingly created a temporal echo—a being with all of Storth's memories and capabilities, but existing in a reality where every good choice had led to a terrible consequence.

The Fall into Darkness

What makes The Empyrean's story particularly tragic is that their fall was motivated by the purest of intentions. Faced with the destruction of everything they held dear, this mirror-Storth made the choice that their original would never make—they sacrificed everything and everyone they cared about in a desperate gambit to save the world.

But desperate times make fools of the wise and monsters of the righteous. In their desperation, The Empyrean reached for power they did not understand, seeking artifacts that promised the strength needed to protect those they loved. The Eye and Hand of Vecna called to them in their darkest hour, whispering promises of the power needed to set things right.

The corruption was subtle at first. The Eye showed them hidden truths that others were too weak to face. The Hand gave them the power to make the hard choices that others wouldn't make. But with each use, each "necessary" sacrifice, The Empyrean lost more of themselves to Vecna's influence. By the time they realized what was happening, it was too late—they had become the very threat they had sought to prevent.

Artifacts of Corruption

The Eye and Hand of Vecna

Legendary Cursed Artifacts (Requires Attunement, Curse Cannot Be Removed)

These artifacts remain part of The Empyrean's divine form, though their presence is subtle enough that those unfamiliar with Vecna's influence might not recognize them. The Eye appears as a constantly weeping socket that leaks black ichor, while the Hand manifests as a withered left appendage bearing the marks of ancient necromancy.

Cursed Properties:
  • Terrible Truths: The Eye reveals hidden knowledge, but always the darkest possible interpretation of events.
  • Life Drain: The Hand can drain life force with a touch, but each use costs the wielder a cherished memory.
  • Inevitable Corruption: Extended exposure slowly erodes the user's capacity for joy and emotional connection.
  • Whispered Justifications: The artifacts constantly suggest "necessary" compromises that lead to greater evils.

The Sacrificial Altar of Echoes

Legendary Cursed Location

A stone platform where The Empyrean's followers make offerings not of gold or food, but of memories, relationships, and pieces of their own souls. Each sacrifice echoes through time, showing the petitioner visions of what they have lost and what they might yet lose.

Cursed Properties:
  • Memory Sacrifice: Followers can trade precious memories for tactical knowledge or temporary power.
  • Emotional Severing: The altar can permanently sever emotional bonds in exchange for immunity to certain mental effects.
  • Temporal Echoes: Shows visions of alternate timelines where different sacrifices were made.
  • Spreading Corruption: The altar's influence slowly spreads to surrounding areas, making moral compromise seem reasonable.

Shards of the Sundered Crown

Legendary Artifact Fragments (Various)

Crystalline fragments of The Empyrean's original nobility, scattered across The Greenwold when they completed their transformation. These shards contain memories of who The Empyrean once was—a person capable of great good, terrible tragedy, and devastating choices.

Properties:
  • Noble Memories: Touching a shard reveals glimpses of The Empyrean's heroic past.
  • Moral Clarity: Temporarily grants resistance to corruption effects, but at the cost of understanding The Empyrean's pain.
  • Empathic Burden: The wielder experiences The Empyrean's emotional weight—the agony of every necessary betrayal.
  • Redemption Potential: Some scholars theorize that gathering enough shards might hold the key to The Empyrean's salvation.

The Secret Faith

Unlike most evil deities who gather followers through promises of power or dominion, The Empyrean attracts those who have already lost everything. Their worship is conducted in secret, not because it is forbidden (though it often is), but because their followers are typically people whose desperation would make them objects of pity or scorn.

Tombs of the Forgotten serve as The Empyrean's temples—hidden burial sites where their followers inter those they have sacrificed in the name of a greater good. These are not places of celebration, but of mourning, where The Empyrean's priests conduct rituals that honor the necessary sacrifices while slowly corrupting those who participate.

Sacred (Dark) Observances

The Night of Empty Altars

Winter Solstice

A somber observance where followers meditate on what they have sacrificed and what they may yet need to sacrifice.

The Day of Necessary Losses

No Fixed Date

Observed whenever a follower must make a choice they believe requires sacrificing something precious for the greater good.

The Vigil of Regrets

Summer Solstice

A time when followers are encouraged to visit the Tombs of the Forgotten and confront the full weight of their past choices.

The Feast of Justification

Autumn Equinox

Followers share stories of their most difficult moral choices, seeking validation that their sacrifices served a greater purpose.

Adventure Hooks

The Grief That Corrupts

A village that suffered a terrible tragedy has begun following increasingly extreme practices to "honor" their dead. Investigation reveals that their new spiritual leader carries one of the Shards of the Sundered Crown and is slowly transforming the community's grief into something darker.

The Perfect Solution

A kingdom faces an impossible choice—allow a plague to kill thousands, or perform a ritual that will stop it but requires the willing sacrifice of the royal family. A mysterious advisor has appeared with detailed knowledge of exactly such a ritual, but something about their offer feels wrong.

The Mirror's Edge

Scholars have discovered a chamber where Storth and The Empyrean once met in secret, attempting to find a way to break their connection. The room contains artifacts from both deities, but using them risks creating more temporal echoes—more dark mirrors of existing heroes.

The Last Sacrifice

The Empyrean's most devoted follower believes they have found a way to break their master's bonds, but the ritual requires willing participants who understand exactly what they are sacrificing. They are seeking heroes whose connections to each other are strong enough to power the ritual—but pure enough that their sacrifice would be truly meaningful.

Doctrine of Necessary Evil

The Empyrean teaches that good and evil are luxuries for those who have never faced true desperation. Their doctrine holds that:

The Burden of Choice

Sometimes the only way to save what you love is to destroy it before someone else can.

The Strong Must Decide

The strong have a responsibility to make choices that the weak cannot understand.

Sacrifice as Love

Sacrifice is the truest form of love, even when those being sacrificed do not consent.

Memory as Weakness

Memory and regret are weaknesses that prevent necessary action.

Pure Ends

The ends always justify the means, but only if the ends are pure.

Divine Portfolio

Domains: Dying, Chaos, Evil, Lore
Alignment: Chaotic Evil
Symbol: A shattered crown above an altar of bones
Favored Weapon: Scythe, Dagger

Typical Worshipers

  • Those who have lost everything
  • Military leaders facing impossible choices
  • The desperate and broken
  • Necromancers and death cultists
  • Anyone willing to sacrifice for power
  • Those seeking someone to blame

Signs of Corruption

  • Increasing isolation from loved ones
  • Moral choices becoming complex calculations
  • Emotional numbing and loss of joy
  • Certainty in making "optimal" choices
  • Important decisions made in secret
  • Black ichor appearing on surfaces

Whispering Shadows

The Empyrean rarely appears directly. Instead, their influence manifests through artifacts that appear to those facing impossible choices:

The Bloodstained Blade

A sword that promises to end a war if the wielder kills someone they love.

The Healing Chalice

A cup that can cure any disease, but only if filled with the life force of innocents.

The Mirror of Futures

Shows possible futures, but only the darkest ones where the viewer's choices lead to tragedy.

Divine Relationships

The Silence

Kindred Corruption

Shares understanding of necessary sacrifice, though The Silence operates through elimination while The Empyrean works through grand gestures.

The Warmaster

Philosophical Opposition

Both understand sacrifice in conflict, but The Warmaster pursues order through violence while The Empyrean sacrifices order itself.

The Ruin

Competing Destruction

Both destroy bonds, but The Ruin acts from spite while The Empyrean acts from misguided love.

Swennlau Hogivoathok

Painful Envy

Envies and resents the neutral god's balance—everything The Empyrean lost when forced to choose extremes.

Storth Cleartree

Tragic Mirror

The original—a bond that can never be severed, a constant reminder of what was lost and can never be regained.

Corruption Mechanics

Whispered Temptations

Moral choices gain effective but questionable options that work as advertised but carry hidden costs.

Sacrificial Power

Willingly sacrificing precious things grants temporary power but increases susceptibility to future corruption.

Corruption Saves

Extended exposure requires Wisdom saves to avoid alignment shifts that feel logical to the character.

Weight of Regret

Areas touched by The Empyrean's power force visitors to confront their own regrets and unmade choices.
