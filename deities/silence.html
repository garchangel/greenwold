The Silence

The Void Within, The Soundless Death

"..." — The only words The Silence has ever spoken

The Hollow Birth

In the cosmic wound left by the chronomancy spell that shattered time itself, something was born that should never have existed—not a being, but an absence. The Silence emerged as Dongus Peardrum's temporal echo, but where the original contained friendship, loyalty, moral restraint, and the capacity for growth, this shadow held only void. Every quality that had redeemed Dongus, every bond that had saved him from his dark destiny, every moment of hesitation that had stayed his hand—all of it was simply missing, leaving behind a perfectly efficient instrument of death.

The Silence possesses all of Dongus's skills, all of his tactical brilliance, all of his supernatural abilities, but none of his humanity. They are what the feared assassin would have become if no friends had ever reached out, if no moment of doubt had ever stayed their hand, if no capacity for love had ever complicated their calculations. They are not driven by rage, greed, or ambition—such emotions require a self to feel them. Instead, they operate with the terrifying purity of a natural disaster, eliminating targets with the same dispassion that winter kills flowers.

Most horrifying of all, The Silence makes no sound. No footsteps, no breathing, no whisper of blade through air. They move through the world as a void in the fabric of reality itself, and those who encounter them often don't realize they're in danger until the silence around them becomes absolute—and eternal.

The Nature of Nothing

Understanding The Silence requires understanding what they are not. They are not cruel—cruelty requires deriving pleasure from suffering, and The Silence feels nothing. They are not angry—anger is passion, and The Silence is the absence of all passion. They are not even truly evil in the way mortals understand the concept, because evil suggests motivation, and The Silence acts only from perfect, emotionless efficiency.

This absence of humanity makes them more terrifying than any raging demon or scheming devil. Their draconic aspect manifests not as storms of emotion like Dongus, but as zones of absolute silence where even the beating of hearts becomes muted. Where Dongus's planning served to protect those he cared about, The Silence's identical tactical genius serves only to identify the most efficient method of elimination.

They maintain connections to the same proto-gods that once whispered to Dongus, but where those entities offered Dongus power in exchange for embracing darkness, they simply use The Silence as an instrument. The Bray Mother finds them the perfect tool—utterly reliable, completely without mercy, and incapable of the moral growth that ruined their plans for Dongus.

Instruments of the Void

The Soundless Blade

Legendary Cursed Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature with 0 Empathy)

Forged from the absence where The Silence's compassion should have been, this weapon appears as a jagged, broken dagger that seems to absorb light and sound around it. The blade makes no noise when drawn, leaves wounds that don't bleed or scream, and kills so quietly that even the victim's soul departs in silence.

Cursed Properties:
  • Silent Death: Attacks with this weapon make no sound, and victims cannot cry out or call for help, even magically.
  • Emotional Void: Each successful hit permanently reduces the target's capacity for emotional connection (Charisma damage that cannot be healed).
  • Betrayal's Edge: Deals double damage to creatures who trust the wielder, and automatically crits against allies or loved ones.
  • Soul Silence: Creatures killed by this blade cannot be brought back to life by any means—their connection to existence itself is severed.

The Cloak of Forgotten Bonds

Legendary Cursed Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature who has betrayed someone who loved them)

Woven from the memories of every friendship The Silence never formed and every loyalty they never felt, this cloak renders its wearer forgettable and ultimately invisible to emotional connection. Even lovers forget why they cared, parents forget their children's names, and heroes forget their oaths when near the wearer.

Cursed Properties:
  • Memory Erosion: Creatures within 30 feet gradually forget their emotional connections to others over the course of an hour.
  • Invisible to Love: The wearer cannot be detected by spells or abilities that rely on emotional bonds or protective instincts.
  • Loyalty's Bane: Creatures who would normally protect or aid each other instead feel only indifference when the wearer is present.
  • The Forgotten Death: When the wearer kills someone, witnesses forget not just the murder, but that the victim ever existed.

The Heart of Hollow Dragons

Legendary Cursed Artifact (Requires Attunement by a creature who feels no empathy)

The crystallized absence where The Silence's draconic heart should beat, this artifact appears as a perfect sphere of absolute darkness that seems to exist slightly outside of reality. It grants the terrible power of the storm without any of the passion that should drive it.

Cursed Properties:
  • Silent Storms: Can summon devastating weather that makes no sound—lightning that doesn't thunder, rain that doesn't patter, wind that doesn't howl.
  • Emotional Vacuum: Creates zones where all emotion is drained from creatures, leaving them as empty as The Silence itself.
  • Proto-God's Whisper: Can commune with the Bray Mother and other ancient powers, but each use further hollows out the user's capacity for connection.
  • Dragon's Silence: Grants the ability to move completely without sound, leaving no trace of passage, and becoming invisible to all forms of detection based on life or emotion.

The Doctrine of Necessary Silence

The Silence does not preach or teach in any conventional sense—they have no need to convert followers, no desire to spread their influence, no grand plans for the world. However, their existence serves as a living doctrine for those who find themselves drawn to the void within themselves, those who have been hurt so deeply that they choose numbness over feeling, silence over connection.

The Efficiency of Emptiness

Emotions are weakness. Love is liability. The perfect killer feels nothing, plans everything, and leaves no trace.

The Utility of Betrayal

Trust exists to be exploited. Loyalty is a tool that can be discarded when no longer useful. Bonds are chains—break them first.

The Silence of Success

The perfect crime leaves no witnesses, no evidence, no sound. Even better is the crime that leaves no memory it ever occurred.

The Hollow Victory

Win by removing obstacles, not by building strength. Destroy rather than create. Take rather than give. Leave only silence.

The Cult of Forgotten Names

The Silence's followers do not build temples or hold gatherings—such things require community, and The Silence's nature actively destroys the bonds that make community possible. Instead, they operate through a network of isolated individuals who have each, in their own way, chosen silence over connection, void over feeling.

These followers are often people who were once capable of love and loyalty but were so deeply betrayed or wounded that they chose to cut away their capacity for emotion entirely. Former lovers who were abandoned, soldiers who were betrayed by their commanders, children who were forgotten by their parents—all drawn to the promise that feeling nothing is better than feeling pain.

Silent Observances

The Night of Severed Bonds

Winter Solstice

Followers perform rituals to cut their remaining emotional ties, burning letters from loved ones and destroying mementos in complete silence.

The Day of Forgotten Faces

Spring Equinox

A time when followers actively work to erase memories of those they once cared about, viewing emotional attachment as a disease to be cured.

The Silent Hunt

Autumn Equinox

Followers eliminate targets chosen for their strong emotional connections to others, viewing love as a vulnerability to be exploited.

The Hollow Feast

Summer Solstice

Practiced entirely alone, followers consume meals in perfect silence while contemplating the emptiness within themselves as a source of strength.

Adventure Hooks

The Soundless Murders

A series of killings has shaken a major city—victims found dead without wounds, without struggle, without witnesses. Even magical investigation reveals nothing. The only clue is that people close to the victims gradually forget they ever existed. Something is killing not just bodies, but the very memory of lives lived.

The Marriage That Never Was

A noble wedding is interrupted when the bride suddenly can't remember why she loves the groom, the groom forgets their shared history, and the families can't recall why this union mattered. The Silence walks among the wedding party, systematically destroying the bonds that would unite two houses—but why?

The Mirror's Hunt

The Silence has begun actively hunting followers of Dongus Peardrum, not out of hatred (they feel no hatred), but because their existence serves as proof that The Silence's path was not inevitable. They must eliminate this contradiction to their nature, even if it means destroying every temple, every cleric, and every story of redemption.

The Emotionless Plague

A strange affliction is spreading through the realm—people losing their capacity to feel love, loyalty, friendship, or any positive emotion. They become perfectly efficient but completely hollow. Investigation reveals this isn't a disease but a blessing, according to those affected, who now serve The Silence willingly.

Divine Portfolio

Alignment: Neutral Evil
Symbol: A broken blade stabbing a hand
Favored Weapon: Broken Dagger, Garrote
Sacred Silence: Absolute absence of sound

Typical Worshipers

  • Assassins who have lost all empathy
  • Those betrayed by everyone they trusted
  • People who chose numbness over pain
  • Spies who forgot their original loyalties
  • Anyone who has severed all emotional bonds
  • Those who see love as weakness
  • The emotionally hollow seeking purpose

Signs of The Void

  • Absolute silence descending on an area
  • People forgetting loved ones exist
  • Emotional bonds suddenly feeling meaningless
  • Weapons making no sound when drawn
  • Footsteps that leave no tracks or sound
  • Witnesses forgetting they saw crimes
  • Hearts that stop beating without cause

Methods of The Silence

The Silence operates through perfect, emotionless efficiency:

Memory Erasure

Eliminating not just targets but the memory they ever existed, leaving no one to seek justice.

Bond Severing

Destroying relationships and loyalties that make people strong, leaving them isolated and vulnerable.

Emotional Void

Spreading numbness and hollow efficiency as a cure for the "weakness" of feeling.

Perfect Betrayal

Using trust and love as weapons, striking precisely when victims are most vulnerable.

Divine Relationships

The Empyrean

Mutual Utility

Both serve to eliminate inconveniences, though The Empyrean acts from misguided love while The Silence acts from perfect emptiness.

The Warmaster

Professional Respect

The Warmaster's strategic betrayals complement The Silence's elimination of witnesses and complications.

The Ruin

Competing Destruction

Both destroy bonds, but The Ruin acts from emotional pain while The Silence acts from the absence of all emotion.

Swennlau Hogivoathok

Indifference

The neutral god represents balance; The Silence represents absolute void. They coexist because The Silence feels nothing toward anyone.

Dongus Peardrum

Perfect Opposition

The original—everything The Silence lacks. Not hatred but the opposite: complete absence of the bonds and growth that defined Dongus.

Void Mechanics

Emotional Drain

Extended exposure to The Silence's influence causes characters to lose emotional connections and empathy.

Memory Void

Actions taken under The Silence's influence may be forgotten by witnesses or even the characters themselves.

Silent Death

Death effects from The Silence's power make no sound and leave no trace, making investigation nearly impossible.

Bond Severance

The Silence can permanently damage relationships and loyalties between characters, mechanically reducing teamwork bonuses.
